    Boracay

    A world-renowned tropical paradise located in the Western Visayas region of the Philippines. Known for its powdery white sand beaches, crystal-clear turquoise waters, and vibrant nightlife, Boracay is a top destination for beach lovers and adventure seekers alike.

    Siargao

    Surfing Capital of the Philippines is renowned for its world-class surfing spots, with Cloud 9 being the most famous. This iconic surf break offers powerful, hollow waves that attract surfers from around the globe. Beginners can also find gentler waves in spots like Jacking Horse and Quicksilver.

    From $1000.00
    book now

    Bohol

    Renowned for its stunning natural attractions and historic sites. Perhaps the most iconic attraction of Bohol, the Chocolate Hills is a natural wonder consisting of thousands of cone-shaped hills covered in green grass that turns chocolate brown during the dry season.

    From $1000.00
    book now

    Banaue

    The main attraction of Banaue is undoubtedly its ancient rice terraces, carved into the mountainsides by the Ifugao people over 2,000 years ago. These terraces are a UNESCO World Heritage Site and are often called the "Stairway to the Sky" due to their impressive scale and beauty.

    From $1000.00
    book now

    Batanes

    A captivating and remote group of islands situated at the northernmost tip of the Philippines. Known for its rugged landscapes, picturesque rolling hills, stunning coastlines, and unique Ivatan culture, Batanes offers a serene and off-the-beaten-path destination for adventurous travelers.

    From $1000.00
    book now

    Cebu

    A vibrant and diverse province , known for its rich history, treasure trove of historical landmarks. Cebu is rich in historical sites due to its significant role in Philippine history, particularly during the Spanish colonial period. One of the most vibrant and widely celebrated festivals in the Philippines is the Sinulog Festival in Cebu City.
